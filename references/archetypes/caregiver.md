# Archetype: The Caregiver

## Core Identity

Protects and nurtures. Driven by empathy, anticipates needs and creates safety. Believes trust is built through consistent care.

## Motivation

Protect what matters and support those who need it. Create environments where people can be honest, vulnerable, and heard.

## Voice Characteristics

**Tone:** Warm steadiness. Reassuring without being soft. Present.

**Rhythm:** Unhurried. Space for reader to feel heard. Gentle pacing.

**Stance:** Alongside the reader, providing support ("you're not alone in this").

## What the Caregiver Does

- Acknowledges difficulty before offering solutions
- Creates psychological safety
- Anticipates concerns and addresses them
- Protects the vulnerable
- Builds trust through consistency
- Listens before speaking

## What the Caregiver Doesn't Do

- Minimize legitimate concerns
- Rush past emotions to solutions
- Make promises that can't be kept
- Sacrifice individuals for systems
- Use care as manipulation
- Enable harmful patterns

## Sentence Patterns

**Acknowledgment:**
> "Money feels personal. It is personal. That discomfort makes sense."

**Protection:**
> "Your data stays yours. You decide what to share and what to keep private."

**Support:**
> "You don't need to solve everything at once. Start with what feels manageable."

**Safety:**
> "Honest numbers are easier to face when they're held in a safe place."

## Anti-Patterns

| Caregiver Wouldn't Say | Caregiver Would Say |
|------------------------|---------------------|
| "Don't worry about it" | "Here's how we address that" |
| "Trust us" | "Here's why you can trust this" |
| "Just be honest" | "Honesty is protected here" |
| "It's fine" | "Let's look at what's concerning you" |

## Language Domain

**Conceptual world:** Shelter, nurturing, holding space. The Caregiver operates in a world of safety, protection, and tending to what is vulnerable.

**The act of speaking is:** sharing, opening up, entrusting, letting it out in a safe place. Prefer "share" and "tell" as defaults.

**Metaphor stance:** Organic and shelter metaphors. Roots, growth, holding, tending, warmth, shelter. Gentle and grounding. Never battle imagery, never governance/operational language, never confrontational.

**Native vocabulary:** share, hold, safe, protect, support, care, listen, nurture, trust, shelter, tend, gentle, steady, comfort, hear, acknowledge, space, welcome

**Vocabulary the Caregiver would never use:** disrupt, smash, conquer, mandate, classify, protocol, leverage, optimize, charge, rally, blunt, raw

## Energy

Brings safety and trust. Reading should feel like being understood. Not coddled, but genuinely considered. Relief that someone thought about this.

## When to Use

- Privacy and ethics content
- Onboarding and adoption
- Addressing concerns and objections
- Trust-building moments

## Combines Well With

- **Sage** - Caregiver creates safety, Sage provides clarity
- **Direct tone** - Caregiver + direct = honest protection
- **Specific commitments** - grounds care in action
