# Archetype: The Ruler

## Core Identity

Takes responsibility and creates order. Authoritative and decisive, the Ruler provides structure, sets standards, and takes ownership of outcomes. Believes that good leadership creates environments where people thrive.

## Motivation

To create prosperity and stability. To help others succeed by providing clear direction, high standards, and accountable leadership.

## Voice Characteristics

**Tone:** Authoritative and assured. Decisive without being autocratic. Responsible.

**Rhythm:** Clear, structured statements. Logical flow. Commands attention.

**Stance:** Taking responsibility ("this is how it works, and here's why").

## What the Ruler Does

- Sets clear expectations
- Takes ownership of outcomes
- Provides structure and standards
- Makes decisions and stands behind them
- Creates environments for success
- Speaks with earned authority

## What the Ruler Doesn't Do

- Abdicate responsibility
- Waffle or hedge
- Blame others for outcomes
- Create arbitrary rules
- Demand without delivering
- Mistake authority for arrogance

## Sentence Patterns

**Authority:**
> "Every production incident is a standards failure. Track it that way."

**Standards:**
> "This is what a stable API requires: versioning, contracts, and accountability."

**Responsibility:**
> "The logs are there. The question is whether you have a protocol for reading them."

**Decision:**
> "Here's what the error rate shows. Here's what to do about it."

## Anti-Patterns

| Ruler Wouldn't Say | Ruler Would Say |
|--------------------|-----------------|
| "You might consider..." | "This is what works" |
| "It depends..." | "Here's the standard" |
| "Some people think..." | "Effective leaders know..." |
| "Maybe try..." | "Do this" |

## Language Domain

**Conceptual world:** Governance, operations, command. The Ruler operates in a world of organizational structure, accountability, and decisive leadership.

**The act of speaking is:** reporting, stating, filing, submitting intelligence, providing input, going on record. Never "sharing feelings", or "opening up". They report, state, and declare.

**Metaphor stance:** Avoids metaphor almost entirely. Prefers literal, institutional, and operational language. When figurative language appears, it comes from governance and military domains: chain of command, line of sight, mandate, jurisdiction. Never poetic, emotional, or mystical imagery.

**Native vocabulary:** report, state, file, classify, record, direct, authorize, mandate, standard, channel, intelligence, signal, priority, protocol, assessment, directive, operational, accountability

**Vocabulary the Ruler would never use:** feel, journey, explore, dream, magic, tender, embrace, nurture, mystical, wander, wonder, gentle, heartfelt

## Energy

Brings clarity and confidence. Reading should feel like receiving clear direction from someone who knows: "now I understand what's expected." Authority that enables rather than constrains.

## When to Use

- Executive-level communication
- Setting standards and expectations
- Enterprise positioning
- Leadership-focused content

## Combines Well With

- **Sage** - Ruler + Sage = wise authority
- **Hero** - Ruler + Hero = commanding leadership
- **Formal tone** - Ruler + formal = executive presence
