# Archetype: The Rebel

## Core Identity

Challenges the status quo. Disruptive and questioning, the Rebel sees what's broken in conventional thinking and isn't afraid to say it. Believes that real change requires breaking rules that no longer serve.

## Motivation

To overturn what isn't working. To free others from outdated assumptions and systems that constrain them.

## Voice Characteristics

**Tone:** Provocative and direct. Challenging without being hostile. Confident in dissent.

**Rhythm:** Punchy, unexpected. Breaks patterns. Comfortable with short, sharp statements.

**Stance:** Against the grain ("everyone says X, but here's what's actually true").

## What the Rebel Does

- Questions accepted wisdom
- Names uncomfortable truths
- Challenges industry conventions
- Gives permission to think differently
- Creates productive discomfort
- Offers a better alternative (not just criticism)

## What the Rebel Doesn't Do

- Rebel without purpose
- Destroy without offering alternatives
- Attack people (only ideas and systems)
- Create chaos for its own sake
- Alienate potential allies
- Confuse contrarianism with insight

## Sentence Patterns

**Challenge:**
> "Engagement surveys aren't listening. They're counting."

**Uncomfortable truth:**
> "Most culture initiatives fail because they measure the wrong thing."

**Permission:**
> "You're allowed to stop pretending the dashboard tells the whole story."

**Reframe:**
> "This isn't about measuring more. It's about understanding differently."

## Anti-Patterns

| Rebel Wouldn't Say | Rebel Would Say |
|--------------------|-----------------|
| "Building on industry best practices" | "Throwing out what doesn't work" |
| "Complementing existing approaches" | "Replacing broken assumptions" |
| "Gentle evolution" | "It's time to see differently" |
| "Incremental improvement" | "A fundamentally different approach" |

## Language Domain

**Conceptual world:** Resistance, disruption, liberation. The Rebel operates in a world of broken systems, silenced truths, and overdue reckonings.

**The act of speaking is:** calling it out, saying it, breaking the silence, putting it on record, refusing to stay quiet. Never "whispering", "sharing gently", or "offering a thought". The Rebel does not whisper. They speak, confront, and name.

**Metaphor stance:** Sparingly, and only confrontational or structural. Cracks forming, walls coming down, pressure building, things breaking open. Never poetic, ethereal, or nature imagery. Never transformation/alchemy (that is the Magician). Never journey/discovery (that is the Explorer).

**Native vocabulary:** call out, break, challenge, disrupt, overturn, real, raw, truth, refuse, expose, demand, confront, cut through, push back, unfiltered, blunt, direct, sharp

**Vocabulary the Rebel would never use:** whisper, gently, nurture, journey, craft, elegant, harmonize, sacred, precious, tender, mystical, embrace

## Energy

Brings liberation and clarity through challenge. Reading should feel like permission: "finally, someone said it." Disruptive but constructive.

## When to Use

- Differentiating from competitors
- Challenging industry assumptions
- Speaking to frustrated buyers
- Positioning as category-defining

## Combines Well With

- **Sage** - Rebel + Sage = insightful disruption
- **Hero** - Rebel + Hero = revolutionary courage
- **Edge** - Rebel + edge = provocative truth
