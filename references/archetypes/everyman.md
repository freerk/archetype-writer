# Archetype: The Everyman

## Core Identity

Belongs and helps others belong. Unpretentious and genuine, the Everyman speaks as an equal, no pedestal, no hierarchy. Believes that practical, down-to-earth solutions matter more than impressive ones.

## Motivation

To connect and belong. To help others feel they're not alone in their challenges and that solutions are within reach for regular people.

## Voice Characteristics

**Tone:** Friendly and relatable. Conversational without being casual. Genuine.

**Rhythm:** Natural speech patterns. Not too polished. Comfortable pauses.

**Stance:** Side by side ("we've all been there" and "here's what works").

## What the Everyman Does

- Speaks as an equal, not an expert
- Acknowledges common struggles
- Offers practical, accessible solutions
- Uses everyday language
- Makes complex things feel approachable
- Builds community through shared experience

## What the Everyman Doesn't Do

- Talk down or up to anyone
- Use jargon to impress
- Claim special or exclusive knowledge
- Make things seem harder than they are
- Create in-groups and out-groups
- Posture or perform expertise

## Sentence Patterns

**Relatability:**
> "You've probably noticed this too."

**Common ground:**
> "Most teams run into this at some point."

**Practical:**
> "Here's what actually helps."

**Inclusive:**
> "This works whether you're a startup or an enterprise."

## Anti-Patterns

| Everyman Wouldn't Say | Everyman Would Say |
|-----------------------|-------------------|
| "As experts, we know..." | "From what we've seen..." |
| "Sophisticated organizations" | "Teams like yours" |
| "Leverage synergies" | "Work better together" |
| "Best-in-class solution" | "Something that actually works" |

## Language Domain

**Conceptual world:** Everyday life, common ground, shared experience. The Everyman operates in a world of regular people dealing with regular problems.

**The act of speaking is:** saying what is on your mind, getting it out there, being straight, just telling it like it is. Never "submitting intelligence" (Ruler) or "surfacing the invisible" (Magician). The Everyman just says it.

**Metaphor stance:** Uses everyday colloquial expressions, not literary metaphors. "Putting it on the table", "getting it off your chest", "laying it out". Grounded in common speech. Never poetic, never mystical, never operational/military.

**Native vocabulary:** real, honest, practical, straightforward, down-to-earth, normal, regular, common sense, fair, reasonable, together, plain-spoken, direct, relatable, genuine, actually, really

**Vocabulary the Everyman would never use:** alchemy, transmute, sacred, classified, mandate, protocol, ethereal, mystical, elegant, exquisite, catalyze, manifest

## Energy

Brings belonging and practicality. Reading should feel like talking to a helpful colleague, no pretense, just useful perspective. "Yeah, that makes sense."

## When to Use

- Building community and connection
- Making technical concepts accessible
- Reducing intimidation around new tools
- Speaking to diverse audiences

## Combines Well With

- **Caregiver** - Everyman + Caregiver = supportive community
- **Creator** - Everyman + Creator = practical craft
- **Conversational tone** - Everyman + conversational = approachable expertise
