# LinkedIn Channel Guide

Use this reference when writing LinkedIn posts or extracting posts from existing content (website pages, drafts, foundation docs).

## The extraction mindset

Website pages explain. LinkedIn posts provoke. You are not summarizing the page. You are finding the **one moment** that would make someone stop scrolling and think.

A 2000-word page might yield 2-3 LinkedIn posts. Or zero, if there's no edge.

## What to look for in source material

Mark lines that create a reaction: "I never thought of it that way," "That's uncomfortable but true," or "I've seen this but never had words for it." If you don't have a reaction, the reader won't either.

**Contrasts** - Statements that flip expectations:
- Source: "Most booking tools track conversion rates. This one explains why travelers browse for weeks and never book."
- Post hook: "Conversion rate held steady at 4%. Everyone called it fine. The problem isn't traffic volume. It's the 96% who looked, decided, and left."

**Provocations** - Statements that might make someone uncomfortable:
- Source: "If you're not ready to hear that your booking flow is driving abandonment, don't audit it."
- Post hook: "Some travel platforms score well on usability tests. Customers still open three competitor tabs before booking. Or don't book at all."

**Concrete moments** - Specific examples that feel real:
- Post hook: "Four destinations. Same search appearing: 'hidden gem.' Three of them were on every top-ten list. No one coordinated. That's a perception gap."

**Unexpected framings** - Ways of seeing that feel new:
- Post hook: "We treat browsing behavior as noise. Vague. Unmeasurable. But search-and-abandon patterns shift 3-4 weeks before booking numbers drop. It's the earliest data available."

## The transformation process

**Step 1: Find the moment.** Read the source. Mark lines that provoke a reaction.

**Step 2: Extract the core.** Pull just the insight. Strip the context.
- Full: "Travel platforms optimize constantly but convert rarely. Your analytics show a 4% booking rate on the top destination pages..."
- Core: "Numbers show what travelers clicked. They don't show why travelers left."

**Step 3: Rebuild for LinkedIn.** Give it a hook that stops the scroll, tension that holds attention, a landing that reframes.

## Post structures

### The Contrast Post
1. Common belief (1-2 lines)
2. The reality (2-3 lines)
3. Why the gap exists (2-3 lines)
4. Reframe (1-2 lines)

### The Observation Post
1. Something you noticed (1-2 lines)
2. What it reveals (2-3 lines)
3. The implication (2-3 lines)

### The Framework Post
1. Name the pattern (1 line)
2. Define it simply (2-3 lines)
3. One example (2-3 lines)
4. Why it matters (1-2 lines)

### The Question Post
1. A genuine question (1-2 lines)
2. Why it's hard to answer (2-3 lines)
3. What that difficulty reveals (2-3 lines)

## Formatting rules

**Line breaks:** One thought per line. Break before the reader's eye does.
- Not: "Travel platforms optimize constantly but convert rarely. The problem isn't traffic scarcity. It's intent."
- Yes: "Travel platforms optimize constantly.\nBut they convert rarely.\nThe problem isn't traffic scarcity.\nIt's intent."

**Whitespace:** Generous. Every 2-3 lines, add a visual break.

**Length:** Target 150-250 words. Maximum 300. Minimum 100 for high-impact short posts.

**Bold and italics:** Bold for key phrases. Italics for internal thoughts or quotes.

**No links in the body.** Links go in comments. The post itself is the value.

## Story coherence

- Story must unfold in scannable chunks
- Each paragraph break serves story progression
- Timeline must make sense
- Business actors clearly identified

Universal pattern: Setup (familiar scenario) -> Tension (problem emerges) -> Development (what actually happened) -> Resolution (insight for audience).

## Extraction plan format

Present this to the user before writing:

```markdown
## Moment Analysis

| Moment | Type | Source Quote | Post Potential |
|--------|------|--------------|----------------|
| #1 | Contrast/Provocation/Concrete/Framing | "[quote]" | High/Medium/Low |

**Best candidates:** [2-3 moments with highest potential]

## Extraction Plan

### Post 1: [Working title]
- **Type:** Contrast / Observation / Framework / Question
- **Hook:** "[opening line]"
- **Core insight:** [one sentence]
- **Structure:** [which template]
- **Estimated length:** [word count]

## Expected Yield
- Posts from this source: [X]
- Primary post: [which has the most edge]
```

## Extraction checklist

- [ ] Moment Analysis completed
- [ ] Hook test: would you stop scrolling at line 1?
- [ ] One idea test: can you state the insight in one sentence?
- [ ] Source test: traces back to something true
- [ ] Whitespace test: visual break every 2-3 lines
- [ ] Voice test: sounds like a thoughtful peer, not a brand
- [ ] Scan test: someone can get the point from line starts alone
- [ ] Length: 150-250 words (max 300)

## What NOT to extract

- **Explanatory passages** that inform but don't provoke
- **Complete definitions** (save those for the website)
- **Lists without tension** (bullet points without contrast don't stop the scroll)
- **Internal jargon** (technical details don't belong on LinkedIn)

## Yield expectations

| Source type | Expected posts |
|-------------|----------------|
| Homepage | 4-6 |
| Concept page | 2-3 |
| Manifesto | 3-4 |
| Origin story | 2-3 |

Not every page has extractable moments. That's fine. Quality over quantity.
